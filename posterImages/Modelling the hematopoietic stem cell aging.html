Introduction

Hematopoietic stem cells (HSCs) represent a rare population of cells residing in the Bone Marrow (BM) at the top of hematopoietic hierarchy. A critical balance is maintained between self-renewal and lineage differentiation of HSCs to maintain hematopoietic homeostasis. With aging, this balance is altered with an increase of self-renewal long term HSCs and a myeloid biased differentiation, which favors the appearance of myeloid leukemias and anemias. My thesis project aims to understand molecular mechanisms that cause this aged-related disequilibrium in the mouse. To this end, we first generated single cell RNA-seq data from pools of young and old hematopoietic stem and progenitor cells (HSPCs), isolated from mouse BMs.

Aging alter hematopoietic stem cell balance between self-renewal and aging

Objectives

  1. Characterize aging of early hematopoiesis in term of subpopulation and intrinsec changes of HSPCs.
  2. Analyze the early differenciation process in young and old HSPCs to find its main actors (transcription factors).
  3. Build a boolean model with this transcription factors that explaining the molecular mechanisms that cause the aged-related disequilibrium in the mouse

Methods

We generated single cell RNA-seq data from pools of young and old hematopoietic stem and progenitor cells (HSPCs), isolated from mouse BMs. We used Seurat (Stuart et al. 2018) and Monocle (Qiu et al. 2017) R packages to repectively cluster the cells and then order them along the differenciation process of early hematopoiesis. We also analyse regulons activity with pyScenic to identifyed the key transcription factors regulating HSC fate.
